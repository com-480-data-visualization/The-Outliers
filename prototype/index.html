Chapter 1

The Explosion

For decades, orbital space was a slow frontier. Then everything changed. 75.5% of all operational satellites were launched from 2019 onward.

In 1974, only a handful of operational satellites circled Earth. Space was reserved for superpowers.

Through the 1990s, growth was steady. The total crossed 400 satellites by 1998 -mostly government and military.

The 2010s brought CubeSats and commercial actors. By 2018, over 2,000 satellites were in orbit.

Then came the mega-constellations. In 2021 alone, ~1,450 satellites were launched -more than in the entire 1990s.

By January 2023, 6,718 operational satellites crowd the space around Earth. The curve is not slowing down.
